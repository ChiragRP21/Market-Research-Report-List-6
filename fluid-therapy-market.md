<p><p>Fluid therapy encompasses various market types used to manage patients’ hydration and maintain electrolyte balance. Colloids, such as albumin, contain large molecules and draw water into blood vessels, aiding in volume expansion. Crystalloid solutions, like saline or lactated Ringer's, are used for general hydration and electrolyte replenishment. The blood market includes transfusions and blood products, essential for treating anemia and significant blood loss. Each type plays a crucial role in clinical settings for patient care and recovery.</p></p>
