<p><p>Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) Film is a technology used to extend the shelf life of food products by altering the composition of gases surrounding the food. This packaging technique typically reduces oxygen levels and increases levels of carbon dioxide and nitrogen, thereby slowing down oxidation and microbial growth. It is widely utilized in the food industry for products like meat, fruits, and ready-to-eat meals.</p><p>The MAP Film market is experiencing significant growth, driven by rising consumer demand for fresh and minimally processed food options, coupled with an increasing emphasis on food safety. Innovations in film materials, such as biodegradable options and advanced barrier properties, are enhancing product quality and sustainability.
