<p><p>Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES) is a surface-sensitive analytical technique used to determine the elemental composition and chemical state of materials at the micro and nanoscale. The method exploits the Auger effect, where an atom releases energy to eject an inner-shell electron, resulting in the emission of Auger electrons characteristic of the elemental species present. This capability makes AES essential in a variety of fields, including materials science, semiconductor manufacturing, and nanotechnology.</p><p>The Auger Electron Spectroscopy market is witnessing notable growth, fueled by increasing demand for advanced materials characterization across various industries.
