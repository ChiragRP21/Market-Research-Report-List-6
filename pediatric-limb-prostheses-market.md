Upper extremity prostheses are designed for children who have lost part or all of their arm, hand, or fingers, allowing them to perform daily tasks and participate in activities. Lower extremity prostheses are for children with missing legs, feet, or toes, helping them walk, run, and engage in physical activities. Both types aim to improve mobility, function, and quality of life for pediatric amputees.</p></p>
