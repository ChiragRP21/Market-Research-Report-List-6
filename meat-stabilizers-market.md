<p><p>The meat stabilizers market comprises various types, including gums, pectin, carrageenan, gelatin, and others. Gums, like xanthan and guar, enhance texture and moisture retention. Pectin, derived from fruits, contributes to gelation and stability. Carrageenan, extracted from seaweed, is used to improve functionality and enhance mouthfeel. Gelatin, a protein derived from animal collagen, adds firmness and helps in emulsification. Other stabilizers include starches and phosphates, which also play crucial roles in improving texture, prolonging shelf life, and enhancing overall product quality.</p></p>
