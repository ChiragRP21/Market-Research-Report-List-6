<p><p>Synthetic quartz for the semiconductor market primarily includes two key growth technologies: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Vapor-Axially Deposition (VAD). CVD involves depositing a thin layer of quartz through chemical reactions, ensuring high purity and uniformity, making it suitable for advanced applications. VAD, on the other hand, uses a vapor-based method to create high-quality synthetic quartz crystals. Other methods might include hydrothermal processes or flame fusion techniques. Each method addresses specific requirements in semiconductor applications, ensuring optimal performance and reliability.</p></p>
