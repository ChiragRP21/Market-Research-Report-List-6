<p><p>Long Fiber Thermoplastics (LFT) are composite materials that combine thermoplastic resins with long fibers, such as glass or carbon, to enhance mechanical properties. The two main market types are LFT-G and LFT-D. LFT-G, or long fiber glass thermoplastics, primarily utilize glass fibers for reinforcement, offering excellent strength-to-weight ratios and stiffness. LFT-D, or long fiber direct thermoplastics, involves directly extruding continuous fibers into the thermoplastic matrix, resulting in improved material consistency and performance. Both types are widely used in automotive and aerospace applications.</p></p>
