<p><p>The citrus-based dietary fibers market encompasses three primary types: pectin, hemicellulose, and cellulose. Pectin, derived from citrus peels, is widely used as a gelling agent in food and pharmaceuticals. Hemicellulose, a complex carbohydrate found in plant cell walls, serves as a functional ingredient in various applications, enhancing texture and moisture retention. Cellulose, a crucial structural component of plant fibers, is utilized for its thickening and stabilizing properties. Together, these fibers contribute to health benefits and food quality.</p></p>
