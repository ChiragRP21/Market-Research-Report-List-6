<p><p>Non-contact extensometers measure the strain and displacement of materials without physical contact, ensuring accuracy and minimizing interference. The laser extensometer utilizes laser beams to gauge deformation, offering high precision over long distances. It is often employed in large-scale structures or materials testing. In contrast, video extensometers use digital imaging technology to analyze changes in a specimen's surface through video frames, providing real-time measurements. Both types are vital in industrial, civil engineering, and material science applications for monitoring structural integrity.</p></p>
