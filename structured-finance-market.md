<p><p>Structured finance encompasses various market types, including Asset-Backed Securities (ABS), Collateralized Debt Obligations (CDO), and Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS). ABS are financial securities backed by a pool of loans or receivables, providing cash flows from diverse assets. CDOs are structured products that pool together different debt obligations, allowing investors to gain exposure to various credit risks. MBS specifically involve securities backed by mortgage loans, transferring the risk and returns from real estate assets to investors, thus enhancing liquidity in the housing market.</p></p>
