<p><p>Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) systems utilize fiber-optic cables to detect acoustic waves over long distances. The market is divided into two main types: Longitudinal Wave Acoustic Sensing, which measures sound waves traveling along the fiber, typically for applications like pipeline monitoring and structural health assessment, and Transverse Wave Acoustic Sensing, which captures shear waves across the fiber, often used in geophysical exploration and intrusion detection. Each type caters to specific industry needs and environmental conditions.</p></p>
