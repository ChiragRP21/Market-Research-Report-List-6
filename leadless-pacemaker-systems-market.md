<p><p>Leadless pacemaker systems are advanced cardiac devices designed to regulate heart rhythm without the need for traditional leads and wires. Single-chambered systems are used to pace one heart chamber, typically the right ventricle, making them suitable for patients with specific arrhythmias. In contrast, double-chambered systems can stimulate both the atrium and ventricle, offering a more synchronized heart response for those with more complex rhythm disorders. These innovations enhance patient comfort and reduce complication risks compared to conventional pacemakers.</p></p>
