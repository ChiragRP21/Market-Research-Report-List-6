<p><p>The intracranial monitoring equipment market is divided into two main types: invasive and noninvasive monitoring. Invasive monitoring involves the direct measurement of intracranial pressure through devices implanted in the brain, providing accurate and real-time data essential for critical neurological conditions. Noninvasive monitoring methods, on the other hand, utilize external devices such as sensors and imaging techniques to assess brain activity and pressure without the need for surgical intervention, making them safer and more patient-friendly.</p></p>
